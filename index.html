Q&A
BACKUP EVIDENCE

Questions the audience may ask

Why use 25 minutes if adsorption equilibrium can occur earlier?

The kinetic study describes adsorption in a simplified standard solution. The full analytical optimization considered matrix, volume, sorbent mass, signal and reproducibility, and selected 25 minutes as the optimized operating condition.

Why different pH values for Cd and Pb?

Adsorption depends on surface charge and metal speciation. The pH screening produced the strongest analytical response at pH 7 for Cd and pH 4 for Pb within the evaluated range.

Does 106.66× mean the result is 106.66× more accurate?

No. It is a sensitivity gain based on calibration slope. Accuracy and precision were evaluated separately using relative error and RSD.

Why is matrix-matched calibration necessary?

Water, tea and coffee produced significantly different calibration behavior. Matrix-matched curves reduce bias caused by beverage components.

What is the largest practical limitation?

Manual probe insertion can affect position, timing and particle delivery. Automation is the clearest next step for reproducibility and routine use.

Can the method already be called a standard method?

Not yet. It has strong single-laboratory validation, but broad standardization would require inter-laboratory studies, independent confirmation and robust automation.
